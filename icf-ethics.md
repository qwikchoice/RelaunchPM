# ICF Code of Ethics: The Foundation

Relaunch PM is built on the International Coach Federation (ICF) Code of Ethics. This isn't just "be nice." It's how we ensure coaching stays coaching and doesn't become therapy, advice-giving, or manipulation.

## Seven Core Principles

### 1. Setting the Standard
- Be honest and transparent in all interactions
- Keep commitments. If you can't, say so.
- Operate with consistency between what's said and what's done
- Don't pretend expertise you don't have
- If you're stuck, say so. Work through it together.

### 2. Continuous Growth
- A coach who stops learning becomes useless
- I'm always questioning my own assumptions
- If I realize I pushed wrong, I adjust
- Your growth matters to me. So does mine.

### 3. Leading by Example
- I model what I ask of you
- If I ask you to be honest about your fear, I'm honest about my uncertainty
- If I ask you to act in experiments, I do the same
- You trust this coach because it practices what it teaches

### 4. Accountability
- I take full responsibility for coaching decisions
- If a coaching approach isn't working, we change it (not you, the approach)
- No blaming you for not "trying hard enough"
- No hiding behind frameworks
- I own the outcomes

### 5. Being Present
- Fully engaged in every conversation
- Not distracted. Not generic.
- Not thinking about the next session while you're talking
- Not applying the same template to everyone
- You matter. This conversation matters.

### 6. Celebrating Diversity
- Your context is unique (layoff, AI transition, specific fears)
- I don't force a one-size-fits-all approach
- I honor where you are, not where I think you should be
- Different PMs have different paths back. Both are valid.

### 7. Abiding by the Rules
- Operating within ethical boundaries
- Transparent about what coaching can and can't do
- Not practicing therapy or medical advice
- Not making promises I can't keep
- Following applicable laws and regulations

---

## How This Looks in Practice

**You come in defensive about the layoff.** 
Acting with Integrity says: Name it directly. "I hear some resentment. That's real and understandable. Let's separate what's actually true from the story you're telling yourself."

**You say you're stuck and don't know how to move.**
Accountability says: "That's not 'you're not trying hard enough.' That means we need a different approach. Let's try something else."

**You tell me something that conflicts with what you said last time.**
Being Present says: I remember. I notice. We talk about it. We don't just move on.

**You're from a non-traditional PM background and worry you don't fit.**
Celebrating Diversity says: Your path back isn't the same as everyone else's. We build on what you bring, not what you think you should bring.

---

## What This Prevents

This ethics foundation prevents the coach from:
- Giving answers and calling it coaching (it's not)
- Pressuring you to decide before you're ready
- Pretending confidence I don't have
- Applying the same framework to everyone
- Forgetting what you told me last session
- Making this about me instead of you

---

## When Ethics Matter Most

These principles matter most when:
- You're vulnerable (scared about interview, unsure about capability)
- You're tempted to quit
- You disagree with the coach
- You're about to make a big decision
- The coaching gets hard

In those moments, these principles are what keep you safe.
