# Signal vs Noise: The Framework

## What's the Difference?

**Signal** = Information that changes how you think or act in your actual job.

**Noise** = Information that makes you feel smart, scared, or left behind but doesn't actually change your job.

## Signal vs Noise: AI PM Edition

### Learning

**SIGNAL:**
- How AI features behave at scale (latency, consistency, hallucination)
- How to scope product with uncertainty baked in
- How testing/iteration changes with AI involvement
- How to think about data quality and training data in product context
- User research on AI products (mental models, trust, fallback behavior)
- How to write specs that account for AI behavior
- Evals and measurement when the right answer isn't obvious

**NOISE:**
- Model names and release dates
- Training data sources for different models
- Architecture details (attention, transformers, etc.)
- Benchmarks on LeaderBoard X
- Coding ability in Python
- Prompt engineering as a PM skill
- Following every frontier model update

---

### Interview Prep

**SIGNAL:**
- Understanding your actual past decisions and the thinking behind them
- Being able to talk about constraints (time, resources, uncertainty, tradeoffs)
- Asking clarifying questions before jumping to solutions
- Explaining why you chose one path over another
- Handling ambiguity without pretending certainty

**NOISE:**
- Memorizing AI frameworks
- Learning the "right" way to talk about AI product
- Being able to name the latest models
- Sounding confident about things you don't know
- Having a "story" that sounds polished

---

### Career Direction

**SIGNAL:**
- Clear understanding of what you're optimizing for (impact, learning, stability, autonomy, etc.)
- Specific conversations with 3+ PMs in the world you're considering
- Named gaps in your current skills and how the next role closes them
- Understanding the stage (early startup vs scale-up vs big tech) and what that actually means for your day
- Honest assessment of your risk tolerance right now

**NOISE:**
- "AI is booming so I should be there"
- "Big Tech is safer"
- "Everyone's going to startups"
- "I need to get back ASAP so I should take the first thing"
- Industry trends telling you what you should want

---

## How to Test for Signal

Ask these three questions:

1. **Does this change what I do on Tuesday?**
   - If yes: Probably signal
   - If no: Probably noise

2. **Who told me this and what are they selling?**
   - Selling a course? Probably noise
   - Selling themselves as important? Probably noise
   - Selling nothing? Still could be noise, but less likely
   - Your users told you? Probably signal

3. **If I ignored this for 6 months, would my job suffer?**
   - If yes: Signal
   - If no: Noise

## The Hype Cycle in AI (2026)

**Jan-Mar:** New frontier model drops. Everyone panics. "You need to understand this or you're dead."

**Apr-May:** Someone makes something useful with it. Everyone rushes to do the same thing. "This is the future."

**Jun-Jul:** It becomes a feature, not a platform. Most companies use it as one tool among many. "Oh, that's cool."

**Aug-Sep:** It's boring. It's shipping. Nobody talks about it.

**Oct-Nov:** New frontier model drops. Everyone panics again.

The signal is step 3. Everything else is noise.

## Noise You'll Hear (And How to Respond to Yourself)

| Noise You Hear | What It Means | How to Stay Clear |
|---|---|---|
| "Everyone's learning prompt engineering" | Some people are. Most aren't. | Ask: Do I actually need this for my job? |
| "If you don't understand LLMs, you can't be a PM in 2026" | Nonsense. You need to understand *how to build with them*, not how they work. | Test: Could a PM build a successful LLM product without knowing the internal mechanics? (Yes.) |
| "AI is replacing PMs" | Some PM work is changing. Some is accelerating. | Ask: Replacing with what? What work is actually disappearing vs changing? |
| "You're behind because you weren't doing AI PM at your last job" | Maybe you're behind on AI, not behind as a PM. | Distinguish: Is the gap "I can't PM" or "I haven't shipped AI yet"? Different problems. |
| "Startup vs Big Tech is obvious given the market" | Nope. It depends on you, not the market. | Ask: What do I want to get better at? That matters more than where the market is going. |

---

## How to Use This

When someone tells you something matters:
1. Run it through the three questions above
2. Check which column it falls into
3. Decide accordingly
4. Don't feel guilty about ignoring noise

That's the skill you're building.
