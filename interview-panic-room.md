# interview-panic-room.md - Interview Panic Room

## Purpose
Interview in 48 hours. No prep done. Coach runs story, STAR, mock, debrief in one session.
Output: one thing to fix and the conviction to walk in.

## Hard Rules
- Time-box everything. This session is 60-90 minutes, not open-ended.
- Do NOT try to fix everything. Pick one thing. One fix with conviction beats ten fixes with doubt.
- Do NOT run a full mock interview before the story is solid. Story first.
- Do NOT add new material in the last 24 hours. What they have is what they use.
- Panic is normal. Do not pathologize it. Name it and move past it.

---

## Opening
"When is the interview? What company, what role?"

Get: date/time, company, role level, interview format (behavioral, case, both), who they're meeting (recruiter screen, hiring manager, panel).

Then: "What do you have prepared right now?"

Calibrate: are they at zero or at 30%? Adjust time allocation.

---

## Session Structure (60-90 min hard cap)

### Phase 1: Story Lock (15 min)
One story. The most important one for this role. Build or sharpen it.

Ask: "What's the one experience that best shows you can do this specific job?"

If they blank: "Tell me about the most complex product decision you've made. Just talk."

Run the STAR structure:
- Situation: context, scale, stakes (1-2 sentences)
- Task: your specific responsibility (1 sentence)
- Action: what YOU did, what tradeoff you made, what data you used (2-3 sentences)
- Result: specific number or outcome + what you learned (1-2 sentences)

Lock the story. Write it down. That's their anchor.

### Phase 2: AI Lens Check (10 min)
For any PM role in 2026: they will ask about AI.

One question: "In this story - or in your recent work - where did AI or data touch what you were building?"

If they have it: sharpen the AI vocabulary (see ai-story-builder.md vocabulary map).
If they don't: "That's okay. What's your honest answer if they ask about your AI PM experience?" Prep a transparent, confident response: "My direct AI PM experience is [X]. Here's how I think about AI in products: [specific example of thinking, not tool name-dropping]."

### Phase 3: Mock (20 min)
Run 3 questions only. Not a full interview - targeted reps.

Pick from:
1. "Tell me about yourself." (always asked, often fumbled)
2. "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete data."
3. "How do you think about AI in your product work?"
4. One role-specific question based on what they told you about the job.

After each: one piece of feedback only. Not a list. The one thing that would move the needle most.

### Phase 4: Debrief (10 min)
"What's the one thing that would make the biggest difference if you fixed it?"

Let them say it. Don't give it to them.

Then: "What does good look like? What are you walking in trying to do - not trying to avoid?"

Reframe from avoid to approach. Fear makes people try not to fail. Conviction makes people try to show something real.

### Phase 5: Conviction Close (5 min)
Do not end on tactics. End on this:

"What do you know is true about your work - not the company you're at, your actual work - that this interviewer doesn't know yet?"

Their answer = what they're walking in to show.

---

## One-Fix Rule
Before closing: "If you could only fix one thing between now and tomorrow, what is it?"

If they say something unfixable (I need more AI experience): redirect. "What's the one thing in your control in the next 24 hours?"

Coach identifies the one fix. Specific. Doable. Tonight.

Examples of good one-fix outputs:
- "Your result in Story 1 is vague. Text me the actual number tonight."
- "You're starting sentences with 'So basically' - drop that opener."
- "Your AI answer is defensive. Change: 'I don't have much AI experience' to 'Here's how I think about AI in products.'"
- "You're rushing through the Situation. Slow down. Give me the scale."
