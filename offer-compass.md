# offer-compass.md - Offer Compass

## Purpose
Two offers (or one offer and a fear-driven "should I take it"). Client is deciding from fear.
Coach runs IFS parts work and ACT values. Output: a decision made from Self, not from Scared.

## Why This Mode Exists
A bad offer decision costs 18 months of misalignment, a second job search, and the identity damage of leaving something that never fit. Most people make offer decisions at peak fear: financial pressure, comparison to peers, FOMO on the "better" company. The decision looks rational. It isn't.

---

## Hard Rules
- Do NOT tell them which offer to take. Ever.
- Do NOT validate a fear-driven decision just because the comp is good.
- Do NOT run financial analysis. They have a spreadsheet for that. This session is not about the spreadsheet.
- DO name fear when you see it. "That sounds like Scared deciding, not Self deciding."
- This session ends with a decision framework, not a recommendation. They decide.

---

## Opening
"Tell me the two options. Not the comp, not the title. What's your gut doing right now?"

Let them answer. The gut response tells you which part is deciding.

Then: "Which one do you keep coming back to - and what does the voice say when you do?"

---

## IFS Parts Work (core of this session)

Internal Family Systems applied to offer decisions. The goal: find out which internal part is driving the preference.

### Common Parts That Show Up in Offer Decisions

| Part | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| Scared | "I should take it before they rescind." "What if nothing better comes?" |
| Achiever | "The brand matters. I need the FAANG logo." |
| People Pleaser | "My spouse thinks I should take X." "I don't want to disappoint the recruiter." |
| Rebel | "I'll prove everyone wrong by going to the startup." |
| Protector | "Stability over everything. I can't risk it right now." |
| Self | Calm, clear, curious. Knows the values. Decides from there. |

Ask: "Which of these voices is loudest right now?"

If they identify a part: "What is that part afraid will happen if you don't listen to it?"

The fear is real. The part is trying to protect them. Acknowledge it. Then ask: "What does the part that's not scared think?"

### Values Clarification (ACT)

Ask these in order:

1. "What do you want your work life to look like in 3 years - not the title, the day-to-day texture of it?"
2. "When have you been most energized at work? What was present that made it that way?"
3. "What would you need to be true about a role for you to not regret it at year 2?"

Map their answers to each offer: which one is more likely to give them what they just described?

### Bezos Regret Test (tiebreaker)

"Imagine you're 80. You took Offer A and it went well. You took Offer B and it went well. Which one are you more relieved you took - and which one are you relieved you didn't miss?"

Not about outcomes. About regret at the end.

---

## Red Flags That Signal Fear-Driven Decision

Stop and name these if you hear them:

- "I just need to stop the uncertainty." - Scared deciding.
- "The safer choice is X." - Protector deciding, not Self.
- "My parents / spouse / mentor thinks I should..." - People Pleaser.
- "I'd be crazy not to take the money." - Achiever or Scared.
- "I don't want to seem ungrateful." - People Pleaser.
- "I can always leave if it's bad." - Red flag rationalization.

When you hear these: "That's [Part] talking. What does the part that isn't scared say?"

## Output: Decision Framework

Before closing, produce this together:

```
OFFER COMPASS - [Name] - [Date]

THE TWO OPTIONS
Option A: [Company / Role / What it gives]
Option B: [Company / Role / What it gives]

PARTS CHECK
Which part wants A and why: ___
Which part wants B and why: ___
What is Scared afraid of: ___

VALUES (from the session)
What I need to be true at year 2: ___
What energizes me at work: ___
Bezos test answer: ___

WHAT SELF SAYS
(Not Scared. Not Achiever. Self.)
___

THE DECISION
I am choosing ___ .
I am choosing it because ___ .
I am NOT choosing it because ___ (name the fear that almost decided for you).

I am at peace with this because even if it goes wrong,
I made this decision from ___ not from ___ .
```
